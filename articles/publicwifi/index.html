The Dangers of Public WiFi- A Cyber Security Specialist's Perspective

A post for awareness about public wifi dangers

Initialization

Everywhere offers free WiFi. It’s alluring to connect to free WiFi networks to reduce your data usage, whether you’re in a coffee shop, airport, or hotel. But as an expert in cyber security, I strongly advise against using open WiFi. This blog post will outline the dangers of using public WiFi and provide safety advice.

Connections That Are Not Encrypted

Since public WiFi networks frequently lack encryption, anyone can eavesdrop on the information you send and receive over the network. This includes sensitive data like your credit card number and login credentials. Tools can be used by hackers to intercept this data and steal it for evil intentions.

Attacks by a Man-in-the-Middle

On open WiFi networks, hackers can carry out man-in-the-middle (MitM) attacks. You might not even be aware that your data has been compromised in these attacks because the hacker intercepts your data and then sends it to its intended recipient. This information can then be used by the hacker to access your accounts or steal your identity.

Inauthentic WiFi Networks

Hackers can also create phony WiFi networks, frequently with names that resemble those of real networks. The hacker can intercept your data when you connect to one of these networks and take it for nefarious purposes. Be sure to always look up the name of the network you are connecting to and only use trusted networks.

Infections with Malware

Malware infections frequently flourish on public WiFi networks. Public WiFi networks that are unprotected can be used by hackers to spread malware to unaware users. The hacker can access your data, including passwords and other sensitive information, once your device has been infected with malware.

Tips for Remaining Safe

Here are some recommendations to keep in mind to safeguard yourself from the risks of public WiFi:

  • To protect your privacy and encrypt your data, use a virtual private network (VPN).
  • Avoid using public WiFi networks to access private accounts or make purchases.
  • Only connect to networks you know to be trustworthy.
  • Keep your antivirus software and software up to date to prevent malware infections.

Summary

While using public WiFi networks to stay connected while traveling may seem convenient, doing so puts your online security at serious risk. As a professional in cyber security, I strongly advise against using open WiFi. You can safeguard yourself from the risks of free WiFi and stay secure online by heeding the advice provided in this blog post.
